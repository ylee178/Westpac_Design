How research insights became design decisions

02 · Traceability
Dimension 01

Spatial context scarcity

"Where am I in this deal?"
Insight
The LOS industry has converged on guided workflow surfaces, not dashboards. nCino's Deal Proposal uses a "single sheet method" — a structured dossier sequenced around lifecycle progression. Moody's Lending Suite describes "dynamic applications, guided workflows, AI-led prioritization." Baker Hill emphasizes a pipeline-led banker lens. — insights.md · industry LOS research (Primary + Secondary)
→V1 Decisions
  • D5 Progress spine as guided workflow surface Industry convergence + Hawthorn River's "bank assembly line" metaphor. Station-specific context, not warehouse inventory.
  • D4 Progressive disclosure · no mode toggle Spatial overwhelm comes from showing too much at once. NN/g: user-triggered reveal beats system-pushed help.
V2 · Reinforcement
Pac surfaces "where you are" contextually as you work, without requiring the banker to glance at the spine.
Dimension 02

Procedural context scarcity

"What do I do next?"
Insight
McKinsey commercial banking research: 30–100 days to banker productivity, driven primarily by tooling fragmentation and swivel-chair workflows. Edmondson's psychological safety framework adds a secondary layer — juniors in hierarchical finance cultures delay questions due to reputation risk. Two-layer root cause: tooling (primary) plus psychological safety (secondary). — insights.md · McKinsey onboarding research (Secondary); Edmondson framework (Primary academic)
→V1 Decisions
  • D1 Dynamic checklist reshapes per deal Reduces tooling fragmentation. AUSTRAC publishes five initial CDD paths — my design mirrors that architecture.
  • D3 Inline expandable knowledge Answers "when & why" without leaving the workflow or exposing ignorance to colleagues.
  • D7 Three-way task ownership Banker / system / customer distinction removes "what's mine to do" ambiguity. Mirrors BizEdge's actual two-sided RDC architecture.
V2 · Reinforcement
Pac proactively surfaces context for unfamiliar items — removing the need for juniors to ask colleagues and directly addressing the psychological safety layer.
Dimension 03

Diagnostic context scarcity

"Why is this deal different?"
Insight
Expertise reversal effect — senior bankers' muscle memory, optimised for common products, fails on atypical cases. The AUSTRAC reform (commenced 31 March 2026) shifted from a compliance-based to risk-based approach, amplifying the need to make variation visible. The regulator itself permits "some initial CDD steps can be delayed where essential to avoid interrupting ordinary business." — insights.md · AUSTRAC direct quote (Primary); expertise reversal (academic cognitive research)
→V1 Decisions
  • D2 Skip as friction with structured picker Regulator-aligned. Captures reason as categorisable data — turns silent skips into governance signal.
  • D8 Legacy ACIP vs reform CDD mode indicator Warns when senior muscle memory might be wrong — legacy customer sitting inside a post-reform framework.
  • D6 Provenance indicators Banker-level evidence visibility. Weight comes from the 2020 AUSTRAC A$1.3B fine — Westpac's specific institutional history.
  • D9 Ready to Submit umbrella score Makes "is this deal ready?" perceivable — replaces implicit judgment with an explainable number.
V2 · Reinforcement
Pac flags rare-product specifics in context — "this is bank-guarantee specific, not in your usual term-loan flow" — directly addressing the variation visibility gap.

Why I expanded to V2 · beyond the brief

V1 makes the product the brief asked for. Three research signals — and Westpac's own public direction — justified going further.

01
Industry trend

LOS platforms are moving toward conversational AI

nCino and Moody's roadmaps both signal in-context AI assistance as the next layer, not a separate chatbot. Pattern convergence across the major vendors.

02
Combinatorial complexity

Too many deal shapes for static UI alone

4 products × 4 entity types × 5 jurisdictions × 4+ purposes × AUSTRAC legacy / reform mode split = more variation than a static checklist can cover. AI assistance becomes structurally necessary.

03
AI capability shift

Enterprise AI is ready for in-context guidance

2024–26 LLMs are mature enough for the Cursor / Claude Code pattern — contextual, judgment-respecting assistance — to translate from coding tools into regulated enterprise workflows.

Validated by Westpac's own direction
"People are going to be surprised by how these sequences are going to be reorganised, resequenced, rewired. It's not just going to be a case of adding in AI-specific points; it's actually reimagining the process so that you can actually optimise it to leverage AI and all the various capabilities."
Dr Martin Anderson · Head of Technology & Business Lending, Westpac
AWS Financial Services Symposium, Sydney · early 2026 Source: iTnews article 620401
V1 → V2 V1 makes the product the brief asked for. V2 shows where Anderson's vision points next — reimagined process, not a checklist with AI bolted on.
